Propose a Talk for the HOT Summit 2015

The first HOT Summit will provide an opportunity for anyone involved in humanitarian mapping projects to present, share and discuss their ideas, experiences, skills and knowledge with the HOT community. The HOT project relies on a worldwide collaboration of volunteers and professionals from many different backgrounds and areas of experience and we want the first HOT Summit to reflect and benefit from that diversity as well.



Important Dates

Call for participation announcement: February 25th 2015
Deadline to submit your proposal: March 15th 2015
Notification of accepted proposals: March 25th 2015
Summit dates: April 30th - May 2nd 2015

Contribution Types and Ideas

We invite you to submit proposals for any type of contribution from half day workshops to traditional oral presentations, 5 minutes lightning talks or 1 hour panel discussions. Presentations will be organized by track depending on the types of submissions we receive. Here are some topics we came up with, but we also and most of all want to hear your ideas:

  • HOT and OSM in the classroom, teaching humanitarian mapping
  • Disaster response mapping and using the HOT Tasking Manager
  • Mapping in the field, ground validation, Field Papers
  • Advanced editing with JOSM and iD
  • DIY aerial mapping: UAVs, drones, balloons, kites
  • Sharing imagery during emergencies, OpenAerialMap, imagery tasking
  • Community building, how to build and maintain mapping communities
  • The Missing Maps project
  • Exporting data from OSM, using Overpass and other tools
  • Using satellite and aerial imagery for automatic feature extraction
  • Web development with MapBox and other JavaScript libraries
  • Sharing HOT experiences from your country and local community
  • Experiences from response and preparedness projects
